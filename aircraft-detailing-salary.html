Roles and Responsibilities of Aircraft Detailers


Aircraft Detailers are responsible for the maintenance and upkeep of aircraft in airports or aerodromes. They must ensure that the aircraft is kept in a clean and safe condition at all times. It is their role to inspect (and often repair) any damage or issues on the exterior of the plane such as cracks, dents, gouges, paint chips etc. They also must check inside the cabin to make sure everything is in working order, including lights, seats and other fittings.

But this job isn't just about cleaning; Aircraft Detailers must also be knowledgeable about safety regulations and procedures and be able to identify potential problems with an aircraft's structure or systems quickly! This requires them to have a good understanding of how planes work and what needs to be done to keep them running smoothly. Additionally, they may need to perform minor repairs or servicing if needed.

Furthermore, Aircraft Detailers need to be physically fit due to the manual labour involved with this job (lifting heavy baggage, climbing ladders etc). And despite it being an outdoor job with long hours during peak periods, salary rates for Aircraft Detailers can vary greatly depending on location and experience. On average however, most earn between $25-$30 per hour - not bad compared with some other professions!

To sum up; Roles and Responsibilities of Aircraft Detailers include inspecting and repairing exterior surfaces of planes, maintaining the interior cabin fittings such as lights & seats; knowledge of safety regulations & procedures; potential problem identification; minor repairs/servicing when necessary; physical fitness from manual labour; and varying salary rates depending on region & experience. All-in-all it's a demanding but rewarding career choice!
