Safety guidelines for aircraft cabin cleaners is an important manual to follow. Ensuring the safety of both passengers and crew members is paramount when performing this task! Cabin cleaning includes a variety of tasks, such as dusting, vacuuming, emptying trash cans and wiping down surfaces (including lavatories). However, it also involves handling hazardous materials (e.g. biohazardous waste), which necessitates following specific procedures to minimise any risk of contamination.

Firstly, (cleaning personnel) should always wear protective clothing such as gloves, aprons and masks when dealing with any potentially dangerous substances. Additionally, they should be trained in how to properly dispose of these materials in order to prevent spread of infection or injury. Furthermore, no sharp objects or tools should be used for cleaning purposes; only soft-bristled brushes and damp cloths are acceptable! It's essential that workers familiarize themselves with any airline's specific safety regulations (and adhere strictly to them).

Secondly, staff must take extra precaution when working in the lavatories due to their potential for bacteria growth; always use disposable wipes or sanitizing sprays on all surfaces during each visit. Additionally, it's imperative that all spills are immediately cleaned up - using appropriate supplies - before passengers board the plane! Finally, never leave any chemical bottles unattended; they should be stored away securely after every use.

In conclusion, adhering to these basic safety guidelines will help ensure a safe environment for everyone onboard an aircraft; from passengers and crew members alike! The importance of following proper protocol when cleaning aircraft cabins cannot be underestimated!
